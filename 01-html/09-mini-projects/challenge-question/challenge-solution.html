Ada Lovelace

The pioneering mathematician often credited as the first computer programmer.

Why she matters

Ada Lovelace wrote detailed notes on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine and recognized that machines could go beyond arithmetic to process symbols and ideas.

Her thinking helped shape the future of programming and computing.

Timeline of accomplishments

  • 1815 - Born in London.
  • 1833 - Met Charles Babbage and became interested in the Analytical Engine.
  • 1843 - Published notes describing the machine's potential and an algorithm for it.
  • 1953 - Her work was republished and recognized more broadly by computing historians.
